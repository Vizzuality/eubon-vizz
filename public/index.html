Euro LST

Temperature is an indicator of the climatic conditions which affect the butterflies. So we can use temperature datasets to study changes in butterfly and other species. In particular datasets such as EuroLST, offer high spatio-temporal data resolution, enabling us to do work on this topic with new detail and precision.

Why Butterflies?

Because butterflies are important to measure impacts of climate and sensitive to it.

What do they do?

Some species are pests, such as the cabbage white. Some species are migratory and vary chaotically. Some are local and are experiencing changes in their habitat range due to global warming and climate change. All of these can be investigated using a mix of climate data, observations, and species data.

Which ones might you see?

While it is difficult to get a good idea of some types of butterfly population changes with observations, as these data are inconsistent in the way they are collected, we can get an idea about so-called relative abundance. These data let us know how common different types of species are in an area. So it will let you know that, if you see a butterfly in a given place, what the chances of it being a particular type are. We can also use these data to see how that probability changes over time, and get an indication of the species dynamics.

Conclusion

By using the EuroLST maps together with other datasets we can learn a lot about our nature. This and other outputs of EUBON will make you happy.
